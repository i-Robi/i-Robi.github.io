IceBreaker

Meet like-minded people near you

Prototyping

Significant time was spent creating storyboards and brainstorming ideas. We initially prototyped two versions of the app on paper: the user-centric variant displayed users around you, while the interest-based version displayed social network accounts related to particular interests. The first round of user testing clearly showed that people were more interested in the first approach since the goal was to meet new people.

Home screen design

With the majority of the user's time spent in the home screen, we developed numerous versions of it. One of them was a uniform grid that displayed nearby users sorted by number of interests. The one that we ended up using was a dynamic grid that diplays user's pictures with varying sizes. An original algorithm optimizes the way those pictures fill the screen, and suggests relevant users based on common interests, friends, and distance.

User testing

Design decisions throughout the app were made gathering and analyzing critique from two different methods of user testing. In-person testing gave us general feedback about the interaction flow, use cases, overall relevance of the app, and avoidable mistakes thanks to the heuristics. Online testing, including click and time spent metrics, gave us insightful data about the layout relevance of two versions of the same pages.
