Expert Crowdsourcing with Flash Teams

Stanford Unversity, 2013

Flash Teams are modular computational workflows that coordinate crowd experts. Flash Teams can grow and shrink on demand, and be recombined into large ephemeral organizations. They push the complexity barriers of current crowdsourcing approaches, enabling more complex, creative or open-ended projects. Advised by Michael Bernstein.

YouMote

Stanford Unversity, 2012

YouMote brings natural user interfaces to the TV. Using Microsoft Kinect, we interpret your gestures and voice commands to perform relevant actions on the TV, such as pausing the show when you get a phone call. YouMote was awarded the 2nd best project prize during the CS247: Human-Computer Interaction Studio final competition.

Structured musical interactions for novices

Stanford Unversity, 2012

Even with simple instruments, musical novices remain unfamiliar with possible musical goals and how to achieve them, thus making their experience less enjoyable. We find that adding structural constraints leads to better music and may improve user experience. Published at UIST 2012.

Class Picker

Stanford Unversity, 2012

Class Picker is a web application that helps Computer Science students to plan their Master's degree at Stanford. It automatically takes into account the master's requirements, personal constraints and conflicts between class schedules to generate a set of compatible schedules. The app was referred on the Stanford Computer Science Department webpage.

Stanford Student Center

Stanford Unversity, 2012

The Student Center unifies the grading system for Computer Science classes, allowing the students to see all the grades of one class in one place, and facilitating the grading sessions for the Teaching Assistants. As far as my knowledge goes, the Student Center has been used by 2000+ students from at least 5 different classes.

Stars & States

Stanford Unversity, 2012

This data visualization studies the historical progression of the Star-Spangled Banner designs, and shows the evolution of the flag with the years. A cursor allows users to navigate between periods to see how state additions impacted the flag design.

IceBreaker

Stanford Unversity, 2011

IceBreaker is a mobile web application that allows users to find like-minded people around them. The home page dynamic grid presents relevant users based on the number of friends and interests in common. Part of the CS147: Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction class, we made an extensive use of the user-centered design process techniques to develop the app.
