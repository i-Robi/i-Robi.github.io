Student Center

More reactivity for students, less burden for TAs

Overcrowded classes

While being a Teaching Assistant for the Mining Massive Datasets class at Stanford, I noticed a general trend for Computer Science courses: because enrollment rates are plummeting, it is hard for TAs to keep up with the pace, and students get frustrated. So I took the initiative to improve that situation.

Visibility of system status

The first of Nielsen's ten heuristics states that “The system should always keep users informed about what is going on, through appropriate feedback within reasonable time”. The idea behind the Student Center is to transfer this heuristic to the real world: what if the students had a unified platform to know their homework, midterm, final, and course grades in real time.

Simplifying the work of the TAs

On the other side, Teaching Assistants spend long hours correcting homeworks, midterms, and finals, and calculating the overall grades. It requires a lot of organization and cooperation. I chose to use Google Spreadsheet as a database: collaboration is easier and the supervising TAs can keep track of what is going on.

Spreading in progress

The Student Center was very well received by both the students and the course staff, with many positive comments in person and on the class forums. To my knowledge, at least five Computer Science classes from Stanford University have reused it while I was still there. I hope people will keep improving it and adapting it to meet their needs.
