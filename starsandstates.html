Stars & States

Evolution of the American flag and the states in the Union from 1776 to today

Try the app!

The data is in the flag

The short history of the United States has been one of relentless territorial expansion, pushing the frontier ever back. The Star-Spangled Banner has illustrated this expansion throughout time by adding a new star for each state that joined the Union. The flag itself is therefore a very interesting time series dataset that reflects the history of the United States of America.

The slider: the interaction medium

Our first round of user testing showed that we had to be particularly careful about the slider design, since it is the only interaction medium. We changed the initial basic slider to a custom one that would show periods in alternating colors. This led to a better interaction model, that we perfected with additional keyboard navigation.

Instructive fun!

Testing the app with a wide range of users made us realize its educational potential for children. So, we added a box under the slider that would display additional information. How might we make that even more engaging? By adding a dose of fun! We added official alternate flags for some periods with seamless transitions. In particular, check the periods 1837–1845 and 1877–1890, they're cool!

Backbone of the final app

  • Slider: drag the cursor or use the keyboard arrows to navigate between periods
  • Flag: the stars are added to the Union flag as new states are added to the Union
  • Map: every time a state joins the Union, it gets highlighted and pops out for a brief moment
